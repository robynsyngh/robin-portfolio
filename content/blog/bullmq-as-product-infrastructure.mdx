title: "BullMQ as Product Infrastructure"
description: "Treat queues as part of the product contract, not hidden ops machinery."
date: "2026-06-04"
category: "BullMQ"
published: true
---

If a job can fail, retry, or take longer than a user will wait, it belongs on a queue.

Good worker systems make a few things explicit:

- idempotency keys
- dead-letter visibility
- payload versioning
- backpressure

Queue depth is a product metric. When it climbs, the user experience is already telling you something.
