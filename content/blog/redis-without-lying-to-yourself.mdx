Redis wins when you are honest about what it is storing.

Use it for:

- hot reads you can invalidate
- short-lived coordination
- rate limits and locks

Do not use it as a quieter MySQL.

A cache that returns stale money state is not a performance feature. It is an incident waiting for traffic.
